About the Framework Creator

The Calm Leader AI System is built on decades of coaching experience working with leaders who are capable, committed, and often quietly exhausted.

"Most leaders don't struggle because they lack skill. They struggle because under pressure, they lean too heavily on patterns that no longer fit. The goal is not to become a different leader—it's to stop working against yourself."

This framework integrates three complementary components: the Leadership Archetypes diagnostic system, the Calm Leader Operating System for daily practice, and the Personal Telos methodology for deeper purpose alignment. Together, they create a comprehensive approach to sustainable leadership development.

The AI-powered implementation proposed here extends these proven frameworks to reach more leaders while maintaining the personalized, insightful guidance that makes coaching transformative.

Introducing Telos: The Missing Layer

Telos is an open-source framework for capturing "deep context" about what matters most—whether for an individual, a team, or an entire organization. The name comes from the Greek word for purpose or ultimate aim.

What Telos Does

At its core, Telos provides a structured way to articulate and connect:

Problems

The fundamental issues someone is trying to solve. Everything else flows from here.

Mission

The overarching purpose that addresses those problems. Why this work matters.

Goals

Specific, time-bound outcomes that advance the mission. What success looks like.

Challenges

The obstacles currently blocking progress toward goals. What's in the way.

Strategies

The approaches being used to overcome challenges. How to move forward.

Projects & KPIs

The concrete work and metrics that make strategies real. What to track.

The Key Insight

The power of Telos is traceability. Every project someone is working on can be traced back through strategies, challenges, and goals to the fundamental problem it solves. This prevents the common experience of being busy for months without being able to articulate why.

The conceptual path: Problems → Mission → Goals → Challenges → Strategies → Projects → Journal

This chain provides transparency and explainability. When someone asks "why are we doing this?" the answer is always traceable back to purpose.

Why Telos Complements Your Framework

Your Leadership Archetypes and Calm Leader OS answer crucial questions:

  • Archetypes: "Who am I when I'm grounded vs. under pressure?"
  • Calm Leader OS: "How do I handle pressure in the moment?"

But there's a question they don't directly address: "Toward what?"

When someone discovers they're an "Anchor trying to reconnect with their inner Catalyst," the natural next question is: Catalyst toward what end? What are they actually trying to build, become, or achieve?

Telos captures that answer. It creates the "why" layer that gives the archetype work direction and the Calm Leader practices a destination.

The integration: Your frameworks help leaders understand how they lead. Telos helps them articulate toward what they're leading. Together, they create both self-awareness and direction.

Personal Telos vs. Organizational Telos

Telos scales from individual to organization:

  • Personal Telos — An individual's problems, mission, goals, and challenges. What they're trying to build or become.
  • Team Telos — A leadership team's shared purpose, aligned goals, and collective challenges. Creates shared language and direction.
  • Organizational Telos — Company-wide articulation of mission, strategy, and priorities. The "north star" that cascades down.

This scalability is what creates the pathway from individual coaching to organizational transformation work.

Executive Summary

This proposal outlines an AI-powered coaching system that integrates the Leadership Archetypes framework with the Calm Leader OS Plan and a personal Telos structure.

The system is designed to accomplish three things:

  1. Prepare clients deeply before coaching begins — through a structured AI-guided interview that surfaces their archetype pairing, triggers, and goals
  2. Maintain continuity between sessions — through journaling, progress tracking, and automated pattern recognition
  3. Create a scalable pathway to organizational work — by adding Telos as a layer that connects individual development to team and company direction
The core insight: The framework answers how you lead. Telos answers toward what. When integrated, clients gain both self-awareness and direction—and gain a tool that extends impact beyond the hours personally spent in session.

Feature 1: Preliminary Interview

Before a client ever meets with the coach, they complete a structured AI-guided conversation. This isn't a questionnaire—it's a genuine exploration that adapts based on responses.

Interview Structure

  1. Opening & Context-Setting (5 min) — The AI explains the process, establishes trust, makes clear there are no wrong answers
  2. Pressure Pattern Exploration (15–20 min) — Conversational questions designed to surface how the client responds when stakes are high
  3. Grounded Leadership Exploration (15–20 min) — Questions about what leadership feels like when the client is at their best
  4. Trigger Identification (10–15 min) — Narrowing down to the 2–3 situations that most reliably knock them off center
  5. Direction & Goals (15–20 min) — The Telos layer: what are they trying to build, become, or achieve?
  6. Synthesis & Confirmation (10 min) — The AI presents its assessment, the client confirms or adjusts

Sample Interview Questions

For Archetype Identification

"Think of a time in the last month when you felt overwhelmed at work. What was the very first thing you did—not what you wish you'd done, but what you actually did?"

For Trigger Surfacing

"When do you notice yourself thinking 'I shouldn't have to deal with this'? What kinds of situations trigger that feeling?"

For Authentic Mode

"Describe a time you felt fully yourself as a leader—where the work felt aligned with who you are. What was different about that situation?"

For Telos/Direction

"If you could wave a magic wand and have your team operating exactly as you envision—a year from now—what would be different from today?"

What It Produces

By the end of the interview, the system has populated:

  • The client's archetype pairing with specific evidence from their own stories
  • A draft Calm Leader OS Plan with their top 2 triggers and preferred responses
  • A Personal Telos document with problems, mission, and initial goals
  • A pre-session brief summarizing the key patterns and recommended focus areas
The value: Walking into the first session with a client who already knows themselves at a level that might otherwise take months to reach. The intake work is done. The coaching can start at depth.

Feature 2: Personal Dashboard

The dashboard gives clients a living view of their leadership development—their archetype pairing, their stated goals, their progress, and patterns surfaced from their journal entries.

Dashboard Elements

  • Archetype Display — Visual reminder of their default → authentic pairing
  • Active Relief Moves — The specific practices they're working on this month
  • Telos KPIs — Progress toward their stated goals, updated through journal analysis
  • Pattern Alerts — When journal entries suggest default mode is resurfacing
  • Weekly Check-In Prompt — Direct link to the Calm Leader OS weekly reflection

KPI Tracking

The system tracks both "hard" metrics (goals with deadlines) and "soft" patterns (how often triggers appear in journal, ratio of default to aligned entries). It doesn't judge—it observes and presents.

Feature 3: Mobile Journal

The journal is the heartbeat of the system—where real leadership moments get captured and patterns emerge over time.

Core Capabilities

  • Voice-to-Text — Capture thoughts in 30 seconds while walking to the next meeting
  • Smart Tagging — AI suggests tags based on content: triggers, archetypes, relief moves, goals
  • Pattern Recognition — The system notices when themes repeat and surfaces them gently
  • Coach Visibility — Patterns across entries inform session focus

Entry Types

The system recognizes different kinds of entries:

  • Trigger moments — "Just walked out of that meeting feeling drained again..."
  • Relief move practice — "Made the call without asking for input. Felt uncomfortable but right."
  • Goal progress — "Delegated the Q3 planning to Jamie. Actually let go."
  • General reflection — Anything that doesn't fit a category but feels worth noting

AI Processing

After each entry, the AI does light processing—not interpretation, but organization. It might ask: "This sounds like your Anchor default showing up—want me to tag it that way?" The human always confirms.

Feature 4: Quarterly Check-ins

The quarterly check-in is a structured AI interview that mirrors the weekly reflection from the Calm Leader OS—but with more depth, pattern analysis, and goal recalibration.

Check-in Structure

  1. Pattern Review — The AI presents patterns from the quarter's journal entries: trigger frequency, relief move usage, goal progress
  2. Archetype Check — Has the default-to-authentic gap narrowed? Any shifts in which archetype feels most accurate?
  3. Goal Progress — Review Telos KPIs, celebrate wins, identify stalls
  4. Trigger Update — Are the same triggers still active? Any new ones emerging?
  5. Relief Move Adjustment — What's working? What needs changing?
  6. Next Quarter Focus — Set 1-2 specific intentions for the coming quarter
Output: A quarterly report that summarizes progress, updates the Calm Leader OS Plan, and generates a pre-session brief if coaching is ongoing.

Questions for Discussion

These are the questions that would shape how this system gets built:

On the AI's Role

Where does the AI add value vs. where does it need to stay out of the way? The archetype identification feels like a good AI task—pattern matching against a well-defined framework. But the meaning-making of why someone defaults to Steward might need a human. Where's the handoff?

On Interview Depth

How structured should the preliminary interview be? The Calm Leader OS Plan is essentially a guided worksheet. The AI could walk someone through it conversationally, but there's a question of whether that feels like deep self-reflection or filling out a form with extra steps.

On Journaling Quality

What makes a journal entry useful for tracking? If someone writes "rough day, didn't sleep well," that's different from "noticed I was holding tension that wasn't mine in the leadership meeting." Does the system need to prompt for the kind of reflection that's actually trackable?

On KPIs

What are the actual KPIs? The Telos framework uses concrete metrics. Leadership development KPIs are softer. "I'll know this is working when I notice myself sooner" is hard to quantify. Does the system operationalize these or track them through self-reported patterns?

On Business Direction

Does the focus move toward corporate/organizational work, or stay focused on individual leaders? This shapes whether Telos is a nice addition (individual focus) or a potential business transformation (organizational focus).

Suggested Next Steps

  1. Validate the integration thesis — Does the Leadership Archetypes + Telos combination feel genuinely additive, or like two separate frameworks awkwardly joined?
  2. Pilot the preliminary interview — Build a prototype AI interview flow, test it with 3-5 willing clients, gather feedback on depth and usefulness
  3. Define the MVP — What's the minimum viable version? Probably: preliminary interview → populated documents → simple journal → manual check-ins
  4. Explore Team Telos — If there's appetite, facilitate one Team Telos workshop manually to test the concept before building technology around it
